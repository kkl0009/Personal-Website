About Me

As a PhD student at the Florida Institute of Cybersecurity (University of Florida), my work sits at the intersection of hardware security, cryptography, and formal methods. I enjoy building practical solutions to deeply theoretical problems, from provably secure IP protection for integrated circuits to zero-knowledge virtual machines that verify computation on untrusted hardware. My journey in security began at West Virginia University, where I earned my BS in Computer Science (cybersecurity concentration, math minor). With a decade of experience in languages like Java, C, Python, C#, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, PHP, and Rust, I’m always curious about new technologies and approaches. Above all, I’m driven by the thrill of solving hard problems and the pursuit of security you can prove, not just hope for. I'm currently seeking a summer 2026 internship that will help me further develop my security research skills. I'd be happy to hear about any opportunities you have to share. I love discussing my research, so please don't hesitate to ask me about it!
